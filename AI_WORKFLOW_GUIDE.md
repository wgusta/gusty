# AI-Assisted Engineering Handbook 🤖☕

## Executive Summary 🚀

**Welcome to the future of engineering.** This isn't just a guide; it's a survival manual for working with alien intelligence (LLMs).

**The Core Insight:** AI models have finite "attention spans" (context windows). If you feed them a mess, they hallucinate. To win, we architect **Deep Modules**—self-contained habitats where the AI can thrive without breaking the world.

**The Hard Truth:** You are the Lead Engineer. The AI is a high-speed Junior. **You own the code.** If you can't read, explain, and fix it without the AI, you don't own it—you're just renting it from a machine that will eventually hallucinate.

**The Protocol:**
1.  **Architect (You):** Define the *Interface* and *System Intent*.
2.  **Implement (AI):** Build the "Black Box" logic within the interface.
3.  **Verify (System):** The "Skinner Box" (Tests, Types, Audits) catches the bugs.
4.  **Audit (You):** The "Anti-Atrophy Check" ensures you still understand the system.

---

## 1. Core Philosophy: Deep Modules & The Complexity Tax 🧊

**The Problem:** "Shallow Modules" (logic scattered everywhere) kill AI context and human sanity.

**The Solution:** **Deep Modules.**
*   **Tiny Interface:** A simple public API. (Low Cognitive Load).
*   **Massive Implementation:** Complex logic hidden inside. (High Functionality).

### ⚠️ The Jevons Paradox of Code
As AI makes code "cheaper" to produce, you will be tempted to build more. 
**The Warning:** Higher efficiency leads to exponentially higher **system complexity**. 
*   **Rule:** If a module becomes a "Black Hole" (too complex for you to hold in your head), break it down. Don't let the AI build a legacy nightmare you can't maintain.

---

## 2. The Workflow: "Spec-First" & Ambiguity Audits ✍️

**RULE:** Never let the AI write code until the Contract is signed.

### Phase 1: Design-It-Twice (The Architect)
1.  **Discretion Check:** Is this a core engine or a button? **Don't gold-plate a doorstop.**
2.  **Ambiguity Audit:** Is your prompt/spec clear? If the AI asks 0 questions, it might be hallucinating your intent.
3.  **Interface Draft:** Generate 3 radically different designs. Pick the one with the least "surface area."
4.  **The Contract:** Write the `interface` or `type` definitions first. This is the source of truth.

### Phase 2: The Skinner Box (The Builder) 🔄
The AI implements. You enforce the "Gauntlet."

**The Feedback Loops:**
1.  **The Syntax Gate:** `tsc --noEmit` (Is it valid?)
2.  **The Logic Gate:** `vitest run` (Does it work locally?)
3.  **The Integration Singularity:** **Test the Seams.** AI is great at local logic but terrible at global state. You must write tests that wire modules together.
4.  **The Security Gate:** Audit dependencies and Regex. Did the AI "hallucinate" a library? Is the logic safe?

---

## 3. The Anti-Atrophy Protocol (Mastery) 👻

**CRITICAL:** Cognitive Atrophy is real. If you stop "thinking," you lose the ability to lead.

**The Test:**
Read the generated code.
*   **The 3 AM Rule:** If this breaks at 3 AM and the AI is offline, can you fix it?
*   **The Logic Map:** Can you draw the flow of data through the module on a whiteboard?
*   **First-Principles Clarity:** Can you explain *why* the AI chose this specific data structure or algorithm?

*   **Result:** If the code is "magic" you don't understand, **REJECT.** Refactor until it is "boring" and "obvious."

---

## 4. The Behavioral Guardrail (The Post-Chat Strategy) 🌐

**The Future:** We are moving from "Writing Code" to **"Defining Policy."**
Eventually, the interface between your mind and the machine will be so thin that "coding" as a manual act will disappear. 

**The Shift:**
*   **From Syntax to Intent:** You don't tell the AI *how* to loop; you define the *Policy* of the system (e.g., "Max latency < 50ms, Error rate < 0.1%").
*   **Simulation as the New Testing:** As implementations become "Black Boxes" generated in seconds, you verify by **Simulating Reality**. You don't just run unit tests; you run stress tests, chaos engineering, and edge-case scenarios.

**The Strategy:**
The **Test Suite is the True Source of Truth.**
In a world where the code is ephemeral and can be regenerated by a better model tomorrow, the *Tests* are the only permanent assets.
*   **Action:** Spend 70% of your time on the **Verification Logic** (The Skinner Box) and 30% on the **Intent** (The Prompt/Spec). The code in the middle is just a "compiled artifact."

---

## 5. The Evolution Protocol (The Flywheel) 📈

**The Reality:** You are getting faster. The AI is getting smarter.

**The Shift:**
As your skill and the models improve, your focus shifts from **Syntax** to **Strategy**.
*   **Beginner Mode:** Check every line. Design-It-Twice for everything.
*   **Expert Mode:** Audit the *Architecture*. You are now a **Policy Maker**.

**The Danger of Competence:**
Smarter AIs don't make typos. They make structural mistakes. They solve the *wrong problem* perfectly.
*   **Your New Job:** Stop looking for typos. Start looking for **Intent Mismatch**.

---

## 6. The Maintenance & Swap Protocol 🛠️

Code rots. Even AI code. Especially AI code.

1.  **Disposable Implementation:** Treat the code inside the module as temporary. The **Interface** and **Tests** are the permanent value.
2.  **Behavioral Testing:** Ensure tests cover *what* the module does, not *how* it does it. This allows you to delete and regenerate the guts when the next model (GPT-6, Claude 4) arrives.
3.  **Dependency Lockdown:** Don't let the AI pull in "helpful" libraries without a manual sign-off.

---

## 7. Summary Checklist ✅

1.  **Scale Check:** Do I need a deep design, or is this a trivial doorstop?
2.  **Ambiguity Audit:** Did I leave room for the AI to guess my intent?
3.  **The Skinner Box:** Is the **Test Suite** robust enough to be the "Source of Truth"?
4.  **Behavioral Verification:** Did I test the system's *policy* and *seams*, not just its syntax?
5.  **Anti-Atrophy:** Do I understand the *First Principles* of this system, even if I didn't write every line?
6.  **Evolution:** Am I acting as a **Policy Maker** or just a "Code Janitor"?
